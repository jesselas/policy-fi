# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Researchers, policy analysts, economists, collaborators, funders, journalists, and students who need to understand Jesse Lastunen's work quickly and trust the depth behind it. They arrive from search, citations, social links, conferences, or direct referrals and are usually evaluating credibility, research fit, resources, or contact paths.

## Product Purpose

policy.fi is Jesse Lastunen's professional and semi-academic home: a compact public record of research, policy work, SOUTHMOD activity, and AI-for-economists resources. Success means a visitor can read the existing biographical and research content comfortably, move between distinct pages, find publications or resources, and leave with a sense of serious expertise that still feels current and technically fluent.

## Brand Personality

Precise, international, technically curious, quietly distinctive. The site should feel like an expert research dossier or personal academic folio: rigorous and readable, not institutional, self-important, or sales-oriented.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic university profile pages, heavy NGO brochure styling, startup SaaS hero templates, one-page marketing scrolls, dark-mode developer portfolio cliches, decorative dashboards with fake metrics, and over-editorial magazine tropes that make the academic content feel theatrical.

## Design Principles

- Preserve current content. The redesign should improve presentation and navigation without rewriting the site into a promotional pitch.
- Keep pages distinct. Home, Research, SOUTHMOD, and AI for Economists should remain separate destinations, not sections in one long landing page.
- Make complexity navigable. Publications, models, training, and AI resources need clear paths and scannable structure.
- Keep the tone semi-academic. Impressive is welcome; hype is not.
- Use motion sparingly. Movement should support navigation or disclosure rather than create a launch-page spectacle.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast and keyboard access. Respect reduced motion. Keep typography readable for long-form research text, make links visibly identifiable, avoid color-only encodings, and support mobile reading without hiding core content.
